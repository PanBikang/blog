                                                subword tokenization

                                                 

                                                Tokens can be parts of words as well as whole words. How to find subword, the following three common algo are used:

                                                 

                                                 

                                                 

                                                 

                                                BPE

                                                 

                                                Some trick for BPE:

                                                First add end-of-word tokens "_"

                                                (we can differentiate est in estate and smallest)

                                                On the test data, run each merge learned from the training data in a greedy strategy.

                                                 

                                                Properties of BPE tokens

                                                Usually include frequent words and frequent subwords, which are oftem morphemes like -est or -er.

                                                 

                                                A morpheme is the smallest meaning-bearing unit of a language.

                                                 

                                                Word Normalization

                                                Putting words/tokens in a standard word, some examples are shown as follows:

                                                 

                                                e.g.: U.S.A or USA, uhhuh or uh-huh'

                                                 

                                                Case folding

                                                Reduce all letters to lower case, it is good in some scenarios but maybe not good in others

                                                e.g.: General Motors, Fed, US

                                                 

                                                Lemmatization

                                                Lemma(词源)

                                                 

                                                Represenet all words as their lamma, their shared root = dictionary headword form.

                                                 

                                                e.g.: He is reading detective stories

                                                 

                                                He be read detective story.

                                                 

                                                Lemmatization done by morphological phrasing

                                                Stems: The core meaning-bearing units

                                                 

                                                Affixes: Parts that adhere to stems, often with grammatical functions

                                                 

                                                Morphological Parsers:

                                                 

                                                Parse cats into two morphemes cat and s

                                                 

                                                Stemming

                                                Reduce terms to stems, chopping off affixed crudely

                                                 

                                                (a simple substitude of lemmatization)

                                                 

                                                Porter Stemmer:

                                                 

                                                Based on series of rewrite rules run in series

                                                 

                                                e.g.: ATIONAL → ATE, SSES → SS

                                                 

                                                Consider the following example: This was not the map we found in Billy Bones's chest

                                                 

                                                The stemming is done by experts.

                                                Word Representation

                                                 

                                                In tranditional NLP, we regard words as discrete symbols

                                                 

                                                Some problems:

                                                 

                                                * No natural notion of similarity

                                                * may rely on a thesaurus, e.g., WordNet

                                                 

                                                Word Vectors(word embedding)

                                                Representing each word in a vector space

                                                 

                                                Some examples: one-hot vectors and dense vectors

                                                 

                                                We can utilize the distance between two vectors to measure the similarity between two words.

                                                 

                                                Some advantages:

                                                 

                                                * Can capture similarity between words with vector distances

                                                * Easier to process in many ML systems

                                                * Can be larned from data!

                                                Co-occurrence matrices (static word embedding)

                                                Dense vector representation

                                                Singular value decomposition (static word embedding)

                                                 

                                                Word2Vec (static word embedding)

                                                Term-document matrix

                                                Term-document matrix represent the count of every word in a document.

                                                 

                                                Consider the angle between two column vectors can measure the distance between two documents

                                                 

                                                Consider the angle between two row vectors can measure the distance between two words.

                                                 

                                                Word-word matrix

                                                Word-word matrix represent the count of words exist in the same context(e.g., windows of ±4 words)

                                                 

                                                Similar vector represent similar word.

                                                 

                                                This matrix is a sparse matrix.

                                                 

                                                How to determine the size of windows?

                                                 

                                                The shorter windows make more syntactic, longer windows makes more semantic

                                                 

                                                Note that raw frequency is a bad representation because some overly frequent words like the, it or they are meaningless.

                                                 

                                                Solution:weighting

                                                TF-IDF

                                                Term frequency

                                                tft,d=log10⁡(count(t,d)+1)

                                                Document frequency of term t,

                                                dft is the number of documents that t occurs in.

                                                 

                                                Inverse document frequency(idf)

                                                idft=log10⁡(Ndft)

                                                Where N is the total number of ducuments in the collection

                                                 

                                                And the final tf-idf weighted value of a word

                                                wt,d=tft,d×idft

                                                 

                                                PMI

                                                PMI(pointwise mutual information) measuere that if they were correlated

                                                PMI=P(X,Y)P(X)P(Y)

                                                PMI ranges from (−∞,+∞)

                                                But negative values are problematic, because some low probability is unreliable and also, peoples are not good at "uncorrelated"

                                                 

                                                Sow we consider positive PMI (PPMI) finally

                                                PPMI=max{PMI,0}

                                                 

                                                Weighting PMI

                                                PMI is biased toward infrequent events

                                                Two solutions:

                                                 

                                                • manually add rare words
                                                • add k-smoothing(Laplace smoothing)

                                                 

                                                Dense vector

                                                 

                                                SVD

                                                Approximate an N-dimensional dataset using fewer dimensions. We want to decomposite any w×c matrix X equals the product of 3 matrices

                                                X=WSC

                                                where S is a diagonal matrix

                                                 

                                                Latent Semantic Analysis(LSA)

                                                SVD applied to term-document matrix, which can also be applied to term-term matrix

                                                 

                                                Advantage of LSA

                                                • denoising
                                                • Better generalizability
                                                • Fewer dimensions: smaller subsequent models and easier to train.

                                                 

                                                Word2Vec

                                                Word2Vec is a neural word embedding method.

                                                Advantages

                                                It can be integrated into end-to-end neural systems.

                                                 

                                                It can become more meaningful through supervised learning from downstram tasks.

                                                word2vec overview

                                                Word2vec is a framework for learning word vectors. Word2Vec map every word to a vector and keep adjusting the word vectors to maximize the probability of every center word and its context.
